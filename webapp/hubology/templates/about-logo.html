The hub-ology logo comes from small towns

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

The hub-ology logo is based on 2010 census data for towns in Spartanburg County, SC. The size of each circle (node) in the graph represents the population of a town in that data set. The lines (edges) connecting the circles are based on the distance (in miles) between two towns. The colors for the circles come from the American Flag.

The hub-ology logo is composed of five circles (or towns from the hub-ology graph). The blue circles each represent a developer, designer, educator, and a mentor. The red circle in the center represents a great problem solver in a small town that could benefit by doing more with computers. By surrounding that good problem solver with support and inspiration from the blue circles, we hope that they'll enhance their talents with computing. The logo also represents an "H" for hub-ology.

The graph was created using the Python Programming Language and a collection of really useful modules. networkx was used to construct the graph based on the census data. matplotlib was used to plot the graph and to generate the image output. geopy was used to calculate the distances between the towns.
