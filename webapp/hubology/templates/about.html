The primary goal of the hub-ology movement is to get good problem solvers living in rural areas excited about computers. It attempts to do this by focusing on three things: Inspire, Educate, and Do.


By arming people with skills learned from computer science, we hope to create new economic opportunities for people living in small towns. We also hope that some of these people will give back by doing something great in their small town. If you're passionate about education, you can help out by volunteering to work with local educators to weave computer science into their existing course work. If you're an entrepreneur, you could start your next tech company in a small town that could really use the jobs.


We know the world faces many complex problems. We're confident that the hub-ology movement will produce new groups of deep thinking problem solvers that will tackle the biggest of problems from the smallest of towns for the benefit of all.


Our logo isn't just a random image. It's inspired by data from some small towns and generated with open source tools. Click on the logo to learn more about it.
